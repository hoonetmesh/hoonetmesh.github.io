SIGNAL CONDITIONS

By the RF Signal Bureau

Propagation today was a mixed bag—mostly weak signals with occasional bursts of clarity. The average RSSI held steady at -118 dBm, just below the -115 mark that separates weak from very weak. Meanwhile, the average SNR was -9.0 dB, which isn’t great but is par for the course in our part of the woods.

Think of it as a partly cloudy day with frequent signal drizzle—consistent but rarely strong. Over 1,442 packets were "Weak" (-120 to -115 dBm), and 1,461 were "Very Weak" (< -120 dBm). That’s nearly 75% of all observed packets struggling through suboptimal RF fog.

But all hope isn't lost. Some nodes really shone. Meshtastic 7f00 averaged -107 dBm with an SNR of -4.4 dB, and others like KSG Meshtastic 58d8 and Meshtastic f7fc held steady around -111 dBm with SNR values near -5.8 dB.

Routing was highly scattered today, with 378 packets taking one hop, and the rest widely distributed up to 6 hops. The highest concentration occurred at 2, 3, and 4 hops, suggesting a reliance on multi-node routing due to limited direct visibility.

In short: propagation was inconsistent, but the mesh proved its resilience. Keep your antennas up and your firmware updated.
