🚗 TRAFFIC REPORT

Yesterday’s packet traffic was no Sunday drive—it was more like a New England blizzard rush hour.

4,706 packets flowed through the mesh over 24 hours, averaging 196 packets per hour, but don’t be fooled by that modest average. The mesh has its rush hours—and its midnight marathons.

The AM commute hit hardest between 7–10 AM, peaking at 333 packets at 08:00 with 64 active nodes. But the real show was at 2 AM, when the midnight crew logged a jaw-dropping 460 packets, with 85 active nodes online. That’s over 2,400 telemetry packets and 1,175 position packets, or 80% of the day’s total traffic. These weren’t party crashers—they were GPS trackers, sensors, and mobile nodes having a midnight heartbeat symposium.

Channel utilization hovered at a cool 6.2%, with TX airtime at just 1.6%. Translation: the digital highways were wide open.

We also welcomed 29 new nodes, including Ghost of Ronny Appleseed, Dr. BananaMan, and a retro LoRa pager—welcome to the mesh, newcomers!

But our stats might be undercounting. Our main listener, HOONET (!ba66ff38), clocked a five-hour outage due to a Heltec V3 hiccup. Actual traffic during that window? Almost certainly higher.

📡 SIGNAL CONDITIONS

Yesterday’s RF propagation was like driving through New Hampshire weather—mixed conditions, with some signals shining and others fading into the ether.

The day’s average signal strength was -108 dBm, with an SNR average of -7.7 dB. Signals ranged from a robust -65 dBm to a barely detectable -121 dBm, showing the mesh’s wide dynamic range.

  • Weak Signals: 1,423 packets fell into the "Weak" (-110 to -125 dBm) category.
  • Good Signals: 530 packets registered between -100 and -110 dBm.
  • Strong Signals: Only 707 packets crossed the -100 dBm threshold.

SNR values ranged from a clean +8.0 dB to a noisy -21.5 dB, indicating that many nodes were fighting through interference.

Multi-hop dominated the routing landscape—3, 4, and 5-hop packets each made up large chunks of traffic. This suggests that direct line-of-sight links were limited, and the mesh was working hard to relay messages.

Hotspots like HOONET-1 maintained strong performance with -70 dBm and 6.5 dB SNR, while outlying nodes, like MapleFlask Francestown, reported challenging conditions at -97 dBm with negative SNR.
