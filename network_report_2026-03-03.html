📰 FRONT PAGE: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The HOONET Meshtastic network is pulsing with life once again. Over the past 24 hours, we’ve seen a burst of growth with 71 new nodes joining the mesh, bringing our total to 299 nodes, of which 268 were active within the last day. Despite a 4-hour data gap caused by our own Heltec V3 listener hiccup, the network showed no signs of slowing down. In fact, we estimate that the true traffic volume was likely higher than the 4,542 packets recorded.

The mesh remains healthy overall, with channel utilization at just 1.1%, but activity levels varied wildly throughout the day—peaking during early morning hours before settling into a midday lull. Signal propagation remained mostly in the “fair” range, with average RSSI at -116 dBm and SNR at -15.4 dB. Battery life across the network is holding steady for most, though a few critical nodes are calling for attention.

Meanwhile, mobile nodes like CheeseFish Stick and CatVI are zipping through the mesh, setting speed records and mapping new terrain. And in typical community fashion, the chatter on-air continues to be as colorful as ever—with creative node names, daily nets, and plenty of friendly check-ins.

🚦 MESHTASTIC TRAFFIC REPORT

Rush Hour Redux: Packets Fly at Dawn

Rush hour returned with a vengeance yesterday, as traffic peaked around 5:00 AM with 320 packets—roughly equivalent to 120 nodes simultaneously broadcasting, or what we like to call “the digital stampede.” The period from 18:00 to 05:00 saw an average of 227 packets per hour, making it the most active window of the day.

But as the sun climbed higher, the mesh began to yawn. By 1:00 PM, only 31 packets were logged—think of it as a sleepy Sunday drive through the Kancamagus Highway. Across the 24-hour window, we tallied 4,542 packets, averaging 189 per hour—a solid showing that reflects a network under moderate load.

Telemetry Takes the Wheel

Nearly 40% of all traffic (1,796 packets) was telemetry, underscoring the mesh’s role as an information highway rather than a casual chat network. Position updates followed closely behind with 1,319 packets, while actual text messages made up less than 1% of activity (29 packets). In other words: the mesh is more interested in where you are than what you’re saying.

Utilization metrics show a clear path forward: channel utilization at 1.1% and TX airtime at 0.5% indicate we’re nowhere near congestion. This is good news for future expansion—there’s plenty of bandwidth to go around.

📶 RF SIGNAL CONDITIONS REPORT

Foggy Propagation and Noisy Neighbors

Signal conditions yesterday were a mixed bag—partly cloudy skies with scattered bursts of clarity. The average RSSI sat at a modest -116 dBm, with the best signal hitting -104 dBm and the weakest dragging down to -122 dBm. These aren’t ideal numbers, but they’re not catastrophic either.

The Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) averaged -15.4 dB, ranging from a low of -21.8 dB (barely above noise floor) to a high of -4.2 dB (clean as a whistle). Only 1 packet registered as “good” (-110 to -100 dBm), while 2,034 packets fell into the “fair” category. The rest were either weak or very weak.

Nodes like Meshtastic cfad and KSG Meshtastic 58d8 stood out with consistent performance at -113 dBm and SNR near -13.4 dB—the kind of reliable presence that keeps a mesh humming.

A Note on Hops and Reach

Most packets traveled within 3 hops, proving the core mesh is well-connected. However, we did see paths stretch out to 6 hops, showing that even distant nodes can still find their way home.

Despite the 4-hour data gap, the network maintained its pulse—a testament to the resilience of decentralized communication.
